Manifesto · methodology · why we test 5

Why 1,000 prompts is theater.

Every AEO tool on the internet claims "1,000 real prompts tested." Almost none of them publish what those prompts are. Here's what's actually happening — and why we deliberately do less.

1 · The 1,000 number is a marketing trick

To "test 1,000 prompts" against 4 LLMs costs roughly $7–15 per audit in real API calls. No free tool can absorb that at scale. So what they actually do: ask one cheap LLM to generate 1,000 variations of "best [category]" — then test the first 20, hide the other 980 in an unreadable summary, and call it "1,000 prompts tested." It's accounting, not measurement.

2 · The other 980 wouldn't matter anyway

An AI citation moves the needle when it appears for a query a real buyer would type with real intent. "Best agent for solo founders" is a buyer query. "Top AI tool reviewed in 2026" is not. The 980 are usually filler — synonym soup with no commercial intent. They inflate the report. They don't inflate your pipeline.

3 · 5 queries that match your buyer beat 1,000 that don't

Our free audit tests 5 — the 5 your buyers actually type. We print every query in the report, so you can re-run them yourself. If we test "managed OpenClaw alternative" and you're invisible, that's a fact you can act on tomorrow. If we test 1,000 generic variants and you're invisible on 643 of them, that's a paragraph nobody reads.

4 · The free audit names the gap. Concierge moves the citation.

We don't pretend a 2-minute free audit makes you cited. It tells you exactly where you're losing, on which queries, against whom. The work of actually winning those answers — schema, third-party citations, community seeding, content hubs — is what Concierge does. The audit is the diagnostic. Concierge is the treatment.

What's the difference between SEO, AEO and GEO?

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) wins ranked positions on Google's blue links. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) wins the position inside an AI answer — Google AI Overviews, You.com, Microsoft Copilot. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) wins the brand mention itself inside ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini responses.

AEObox runs all three layers because they share the same content engine — just optimized differently.
